# Community Engagement & Reputation Management

GitHub is a social platform for engineers. Active, high-quality communication directly scales Galaxy Brain and Starstruck achievements while building network credibility.

## 1. Issue Commenting & Triage
*   **Tone:** Highly professional, concise, empathetic.
*   **Action Driven:** Always provide reproduction steps or ask targeted clarifying questions. Avoid "+1" or "me too" comments. If confirming a bug, post the exact stack trace from your environment.
*   **Resolution:** When closing issues in owned repos, explicitly state *why* it was closed or link the resolving commit hash.

## 2. GitHub Discussions (Galaxy Brain Pipeline)
*   **Discovery:** Monitor discussions in target frameworks (e.g., Flask plugins, workflow parsers).
*   **High-Value Answers:** 
    1. Acknowledge the constraint cleanly.
    2. Provide a 5-10 line code block addressing it natively.
    3. Provide a brief explanation of *why* that code works.
*   **Rule:** Quality > Quantity. Provide 2 excellent answers a week rather than 10 generic ones.

## 3. Social Sharing & Visibility
*   When a micro-repo hits `v1.0.0` or a major milestone, draft a short, highly technical "Show and Tell" post.
*   Distribute to relevant Reddit ecosystems (`r/Python`, `r/opensource`) strictly emphasizing the core engineering challenge solved, *not* begging for stars.
*   **Cadence:** Maximum 1 promotional outbound share per month to avoid spam tagging.
